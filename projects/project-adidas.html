Adidas US Sales Performance (2020-2021)

Evaluating year-over-year explosive growth, regional dominance, and the efficiency of online sales channels.

December 2025 Data Analyst Completed
View on GitHub

The Business Problem

After a period of explosive growth in 2021, stakeholders needed a comprehensive breakdown of Adidas sales performance across the United States. The objective was to understand how sales evolved from 2020 to 2021, identify the highest-performing regions and retailers, and determine which sales methods should be prioritized for future investments to sustain momentum.

The Solution

I utilized Power BI to conduct end-to-end analysis. Starting with Power Query, I engineered custom tracking columns (like Operating Profit and composite Location IDs). I then conducted extensive Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) to map retailer distribution before building an interactive Star Schema dashboard to visualize YoY growth, regional dominance, and product trends.

Power Query Transformations

Here's a sample of the Power Query M Language used to engineer custom columns and prepare data for analysis:

PowerQuery 

// Generating composite keys and calculated columns using Power Query UI
= Table.AddColumn(#"Inserted Merged Column1", "Location ID", 
  each Text.Combine({[City], [State]}, " - "), type text)

= Table.AddColumn(#"Changed Type3", "Multiplication", 
  each [Total Sales] * [Operating Margin], type number)
  
= Table.RenameColumns(#"Removed Columns1",
  {{"Multiplication", "Operating Profit"}})

Key Insights

  • The Distribution Paradox: While the Northeast holds the most retailers (23%), the West Region generated the most revenue ($36.4M), driven heavily by the "West Gear" retailer.
  • Explosive 2021 Growth: Sales saw a massive 295% surge in 2021, peaking in July ($10.4M) compared to the 2020 peak in April ($3.2M).
  • Peak Sales Timing: 2020 sales peaked in April at $3.2M while 2021 sales peaked in July with $10.4M, indicating a seasonal shift.
  • Strategic Recommendation: Increase ad spend and promotions during historically slower quarters (Q1 and Q3) to flatten seasonality curves and maintain the strategies that drove the 2021 surge.
